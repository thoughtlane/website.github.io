Tier 1 — stress most

Your Socratic step-by-step solver and real-world internships are your strongest cognitive assets. Inquiry and project-based methods are partially covered — investing here gives the highest return on learner development.

Tier 2 — strong foundation

Quiz with AI follow-up, rubric feedback, and cited Q&A are fully covered and well above industry standard. Peer learning and simulations are the gaps worth closing — real-time co-solving would meaningfully lift this tier.

Tier 3 — support layer

Gamification and multimodal are fully covered and keep learners engaged. Adding a proper spaced repetition engine is the single most contained investment here — it protects Tier 1 and 2 gains from decaying over time.

Tier 4 — limited online

Flipped classroom and social-emotional learning have inherent physical and human limits. Not worth prioritising for an online platform — energy is better spent deepening Tier 1 and 2 coverage.

Top 2 investments to prioritise
1
Strengthen inquiry-based learning
Add hypothesis tracking and structured investigation workflows on top of your existing Q&A and document system — the foundation is already there.
2
Add spaced repetition scheduling
Your quiz engine already tracks right/wrong per learner — a forgetting curve scheduler on top is contained engineering work with outsized retention impact.

All questions, answers, and reports are exportable across every learning mode. Real-world internships with live customers are a genuine differentiator that most online platforms cannot match.
