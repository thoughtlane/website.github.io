Recommended Stacks by Priority

No single platform covers everything perfectly. Here's the best pairing for each scenario.

Priority: AI Learning + Deep Assessments

CYPHER Learning + Confluence Standard

~$1,100/mo total. Best AI assessment and grading with a proper wiki. CYPHER handles all LMS/AI authoring/grading; Confluence handles structured docs, SOPs, and internal knowledge base. Data residency covered in Confluence Standard.

Priority: Unified AI Platform (Future-Proof)

Sana Learn (Enterprise)

$3,900+/mo. The only platform that genuinely bridges LMS + knowledge indexing, connecting to Confluence, Notion, Slack, and Google Drive in one AI-powered layer. Over-budget for 100 users today, but worth evaluating if you're planning to scale to 300+.

Priority: Budget + Flexibility

360Learning + Confluence Standard

~$1,289/mo. Most cost-effective full stack. 360Learning's collaborative authoring + Confluence's wiki depth. Trade-off: AI grading is limited; data residency needs enterprise upgrade on 360Learning side.

Priority: Already on Atlassian / Jira

CYPHER Learning + Confluence Premium

~$1,415/mo. If you're already in the Atlassian ecosystem, upgrading to Confluence Premium adds Rovo AI (conversational search across all your docs) and removes AI token limits. Pairs with CYPHER's deep assessment suite.

Priority: Creative / Cross-Functional Teams

CYPHER Learning + Notion Business

~$2,200/mo. Best for teams that don't want the rigid Confluence structure — Notion's flexibility works well for non-technical orgs. Higher cost, but covers docs + project management + notes in one workspace.

⚠ Priority: Single-Tenant / Own-Cloud Deployment

Moodle (LMS) + XWiki (Docs)

Both open-source and fully self-hostable on your own AWS, Azure, or GCP. Moodle supports AI plugins, SCORM, assessments, gradebooks. XWiki provides Confluence-style structured wikis with dedicated Confluence migration tooling. Platform licenses are free; you pay for infrastructure, hosting, and internal IT/DevOps support. None of the commercial SaaS platforms (CYPHER, Sana, 360Learning, Notion) support own-cloud deployment.

The Bottom Line

⚠ Critical finding on single-tenant / own-cloud deployment: None of the LMS platforms (CYPHER, Sana, 360Learning) support single-tenant or own-cloud deployment. All are multi-tenant SaaS products hosted on the vendor's infrastructure. They offer logical tenant isolation (your data is segmented), but you cannot deploy them on your own AWS, Azure, or GCP account. If single-tenant or BYOC is a hard requirement, none of these platforms qualify as-is — you would need to explore open-source alternatives like Moodle (fully self-hostable, with AI plugins) or open-source LXP options.

Confluence Data Center was the only option here — it allowed full self-hosted deployment on your own infrastructure — but Atlassian closed new Data Center subscriptions on March 30, 2026, and all licenses expire in 2029. If you were planning to use Confluence Data Center on your own cloud, that path is now closed to new customers. Alternatives for self-hosted wikis include XWiki (open-source, Confluence migration tooling available) and BookStack.

None of the LMS platforms are a true Confluence replacement. CYPHER Learning has a resource/content library; Sana indexes external docs but doesn't author them; 360Learning has collaborative channels. None offer the structured page hierarchy, granular permissions, and deep version history of a proper wiki. The honest answer is that for 100 users needing both, a two-tool stack (~$1,100–1,400/mo) delivers more capability than any single platform.

If data residency is non-negotiable today, Confluence Standard already delivers it on the Standard tier ($489/mo). CYPHER, Sana, and 360Learning all push data residency to enterprise tiers — expect a custom quote conversation.

Watch Sana for 2026–2027 if you plan to scale beyond 300 users. Its ability to semantically search across Confluence, Notion, Slack, and Google Drive simultaneously is genuinely a category-different capability — the closest thing to a unified learning + knowledge layer currently available.
